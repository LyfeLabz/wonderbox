The Observerse
▾
The Observerse (Y¹⁴) is the central idea of GU. It's a 14-dimensional space built on top of our familiar 4D spacetime. The extra 10 dimensions aren't random — they encode all possible ways spacetime can curve, which is exactly the information needed to describe gravity and all other forces.
Fiber Bundle
▾
At every point in spacetime (X⁴), the Observerse attaches a 10-dimensional "fiber" — a space of all possible metric shapes. Together they form a fiber bundle: the total space Y¹⁴ projected down to X⁴ by the map π. The fibers you see here represent these extra dimensions hanging off each point.
Gauge Field
▾
Gauge fields describe how forces act across space. In GU, all four forces (gravity, electromagnetism, strong, weak) are unified into one gauge connection on the Observerse. The symmetry group SO(7,7) is large enough to contain all known force symmetries simultaneously.
Spinor Field
▾
Spinors are the mathematical objects that describe fermions — electrons, quarks, neutrinos. In GU, spinors arise naturally from the geometry of the Observerse rather than being added by hand. Each green particle represents a fermion emerging from the structure of spacetime itself.
4D Spacetime
▾
The glowing grid is our familiar 4D spacetime (X⁴) — 3 dimensions of space plus 1 of time. The waves show spacetime curvature, which we experience as gravity. In GU, this is just the "base" of a much larger structure — the Observerse is built on top of it.
