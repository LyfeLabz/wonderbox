Dark Matter
▾
Invisible — no light, no radio waves, nothing. But we know it's there because galaxies spin faster than visible matter alone can explain. It makes up ~27% of everything.
Dark Energy
▾
The universe isn't just expanding — it's speeding up. Something is pushing space apart faster and faster. We call it dark energy. Nobody knows what it is. ~68% of everything.
Baryonic Matter
▾
Stars, planets, gas, dust, you. Everything you've ever seen. All of it adds up to only 5% of the universe.
Expansion
▾
Galaxies aren't flying through space — space itself is growing. Like dots on an inflating balloon: every dot moves away from every other dot.
Cosmic Web
▾
Dark matter forms invisible filaments across the universe. Galaxies cluster along them like beads on a string. The voids between are almost empty.
